MESSAGE: Here's the information on unique customers who used credit cards on weekends for faster-than-standard shipping methods in Germany.  The query aggregates data from sales transactions, payment methods, shipping methods, and dates to provide the total spending.  A table will be displayed showing the results.
SQL: ```sql
WITH FastShipping AS (
  SELECT
    sale_id
  FROM dim_shipping
  WHERE
    shipping_speed <> 'Standard'
), WeekendSales AS (
  SELECT
    fs.sale_id,
    fs.customer_id,
    fs.total_amount
  FROM fact_sales AS fs
  JOIN dim_date AS dd
    ON fs.date_id = dd.date_id
  JOIN FastShipping AS fsh
    ON fs.sale_id = fsh.sale_id
  JOIN dim_country AS dc
    ON fs.country_id = dc.country_id
  JOIN dim_payment_method AS dpm
    ON fs.payment_method_id = dpm.payment_method_id
  WHERE
    dd.is_weekend = TRUE
    AND dc.country_name = 'Germany'
    AND dpm.method_name = 'Credit Card'
)
SELECT
  COUNT(DISTINCT customer_id) AS unique_customers,
  SUM(total_amount) AS total_spend
FROM WeekendSales;
```
